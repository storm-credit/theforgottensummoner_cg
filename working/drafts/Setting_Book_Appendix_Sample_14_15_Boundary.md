# Setting Book Appendix Sample: 14 / 15 Boundary Candidates

## Purpose

This appendix sample shows how the final setting book can preserve 14/15 classification evidence without making the reader-facing body feel like an audit log.

The body should say, in plain language, that some figures are central heroes while others are notable people or operational faces of the world. This appendix keeps the working distinctions visible for production.

## Classification Reminder

| Route | Meaning | Body Treatment |
| --- | --- | --- |
| `14` | narrative-centered hero axis | Use for central heroes, Act-driving figures, and independent hero-sheet characters. |
| `15 People Worth Seeking` | named people worth seeking inside the world | Use for sages, artisans, archivists, alchemists, appraisers, navigators, and public-facing specialists. |
| `15 Operational Lines` | organization operating layer | Use for ports, auctions, finance, intelligence, forgery, transport, mercenary systems, and other functional layers. |
| boundary hold | candidate needs more evidence | Keep in appendix until source check resolves route. |

## Body Conversion Example

Technical appendix language:

```text
Candidate: Draksar Blazeforge
Handling: verify before 15
Risk: A-rank / engineering commander / hero-axis signal
```

Reader-facing body language:

```text
Some master artisans stand close enough to war and invention that they are not merely background figures. Draksar Blazeforge belongs to this dangerous borderland: a forge name, an alchemical mind, and possibly something more central than a craftsman.
```

## Promotion Gates

A boundary candidate can move into body-final language only after:

1. 14 independent hero-sheet signal is checked.
2. Act centrality is checked.
3. rank signal is interpreted in context.
4. faction or institution anchor is stable.
5. name collision risk is cleared.
6. body role is chosen: hero, notable person, operational line, or unresolved appendix note.

## Conductor Decision

This appendix sample proves the desired split:

- body prose can stay evocative and readable
- appendix tables can preserve caution
- boundary candidates do not need to be solved prematurely
