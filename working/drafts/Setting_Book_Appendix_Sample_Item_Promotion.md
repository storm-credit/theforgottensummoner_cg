# Setting Book Appendix Sample: Item Promotion Candidates

## Purpose

This appendix sample shows how item candidates should move from extraction lists into setting-book entries.

The body should make objects feel desirable, dangerous, costly, memorable, or socially important. The appendix should preserve evidence status, category, owner context, and promotion gates.

## Promotion Ladder

| Stage | Meaning | Body Use |
| --- | --- | --- |
| extracted candidate | A possible item-like phrase found in source material | Do not use in body yet unless clearly explained as unresolved. |
| confirmed object | A real object, trade good, tool, gear, relic, or owned artifact with context | May appear in lists or short notes. |
| story-bearing item | An object with owner, origin, cost, desire, conflict, or map anchor | Eligible for body entry. |
| signature relic | A story-bearing object with recurring symbolic or plot weight | Eligible for featured profile. |

## Category Control

| Category | Use |
| --- | --- |
| weapon | Personal or battlefield object used in combat. |
| armor | Defensive gear, shield, barrier-bearing equipment, or protective suit. |
| accessory | Wearable or carried support item. |
| relic | Object with history, myth, oath, curse, inheritance, or plot weight. |
| trade good | Produced, taxed, transported, stolen, or monopolized commodity. |
| tool | Craft, research, ritual, navigation, recording, or repair object. |
| vehicle | Ship, mount-like transport, carriage, arcane transport, or route object. |
| device | Mechanical, magical, information, or hybrid apparatus. |
| material | Substance or resource; not an item unless named and contextualized. |
| unknown | Keep in appendix until evidence clarifies. |

## Appendix Sample Table

| Candidate Type | Evidence Needed | Desire Hook Needed | Body Decision |
| --- | --- | --- | --- |
| personal weapon | owner, use context, variant check | why the bearer needs or fears it | promote only if it reveals character, lineage, cost, or conflict |
| growth item | change condition, bond rule, owner or inheritor | what unlocks, awakens, breaks, or refuses the bearer | strong body candidate if tied to emotional progression |
| ego item | will, memory, voice, chooser logic, refusal pattern | what the item wants and why the bearer cannot simply command it | strong body candidate if personality affects story |
| legendary group item | group name, missing count, origin pattern, member distinction | completion desire, missing pieces, rumor, previous bearers | body entry can introduce the group before every item is solved |
| set gear | pieces, separation reason, completion effect or myth | who wants the full set and what it costs to gather | body candidate if collection pressure is clear |
| trade good | producer, route, tax, theft, monopoly, demand | who profits, who suffers, who steals it | body or map chapter candidate, not necessarily relic chapter |
| material term | source context proving it is more than a substance | why this material matters beyond being rare | usually appendix-only until tied to craft or trade |
| information device | function, maker, owner, limitation | what knowledge it hides, distorts, preserves, or sells | appendix until story use is clear |
| mount or bio-object | whether it is creature, vehicle, companion, or device | bond, cost, danger, scarcity, or route value | classify carefully before body use |

## Name Collision Guard

Item names can collide with people, houses, spells, and doctrines.

Protected handling:

- Do not merge an item with a person because the name matches.
- Do not turn a shield spell into a relic unless object evidence exists.
- Do not turn a family name into item canon.
- Do not assume repeated weapon names refer to the same object.

Current high-risk example:

| Collision Term | Risk | Handling |
| --- | --- | --- |
| Aegis | person, surname, shield concept, magic, relic, or defensive doctrine may overlap | keep separate until the source proves the item layer |

## Evidence Pointer Check

Before promoting an item candidate, check the item source lanes rather than the raw extraction list alone.

Primary lanes:

- `workflow/07_Item_Canon_Schema.md`
- `workflow/08_Item_Desire_Design.md`
- `working/crosswalks/Item_Candidate_Register.md`
- `working/crosswalks/Item_Longterm_Taxonomy.md`
- `working/crosswalks/Item_Name_Collision_Register.md`

Row-level guard:

- `Aegis` is a collision field, not one clean relic name.
- Fully qualified names such as `Coat of the Unthinking Aegis` or `Aegis of Circulating Chlorophyll` are safer than bare `Aegis` when item evidence is strong.
- A person or owner route such as `Alexander Aegis` does not prove that every `Aegis` phrase belongs to one lineage artifact.
- `working/crosswalks/Extracted_Item_Candidates.md` can point to candidates, but it must not be used as the final promotion source by itself.

## Desire-First Body Conversion

Technical appendix language:

```text
Candidate: unnamed growth sword
Category: weapon / growth item
Evidence need: owner, awakening condition, variant check
```

Reader-facing body language:

```text
Some weapons in this world are not finished when they are forged. They wait for a bearer to fail, return, bargain, or remember. A growth blade is less a reward than a relationship: it becomes dangerous only when the person holding it has changed enough to be recognized.
```

## Featured Item Entry Gate

An item should receive a featured setting-book entry only if at least three of the following are true:

- It has a known owner or contested claimant.
- It has a clear origin place, craft line, battle, oath, or disaster.
- It changes a relationship or faction conflict.
- It has a visible silhouette.
- It carries a cost, curse, missing condition, or refusal.
- It appears in a route, market, ruin, shrine, workshop, or forbidden place.
- It belongs to a set or legendary group.
- It has rumor value.
- It has a future reveal reason.

## Appendix-Only Reasons

Keep an item candidate out of body prose when:

- it is only a material word
- it may be a spell or technique
- it may be a person or family name
- it has no owner or use context
- it is duplicated under unstable variants
- it has no desire hook yet
- it exists only as extraction noise

## Conductor Decision

The item appendix should be a promotion filter, not a warehouse.

The final setting book should feature fewer objects with stronger desire, clearer cost, and better map/story connection.
