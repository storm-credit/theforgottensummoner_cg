# Setting Book Body / Appendix Separation Plan

## Purpose

This plan decides what belongs in the polished setting-book body and what should stay in technical appendices.

The current 0-8 draft package is intentionally technical. It preserves audit discipline. The next version should become easier to read without losing the evidence controls behind it.
Final reader-facing label authority belongs to `Setting_Book_Reader_Facing_TOC_Draft.md`.
This plan decides body versus appendix routing, but it does not rename the reader-facing TOC on its own.

## Separation Rule

The body should answer what the world feels like, how it works, and why it matters.

The appendix should answer how confident we are, what evidence supports it, what remains unresolved, and what future work is gated.

## Body Voice Rules

Use body prose when the paragraph can be read aloud to a collaborator and still feel like worldbuilding.

Good body language:

- “Ether is a continent where inheritance and contracts weigh as heavily as magic.”
- “The Crossroad Cities are less a capital than a knot of roads, ports, favors, and secrets.”
- “A relic matters when someone wants it, fears it, remembers it, or refuses to use it.”

Avoid body language:

- raw status labels
- audit table jargon
- file path explanations
- queue mechanics
- confidence codes unless translated into natural language

## Appendix Voice Rules

Use appendix language when precision matters more than atmosphere.

Good appendix language:

- current label
- source file
- canon tier
- unresolved conflict
- next action
- variant names
- promotion gate

Avoid appendix drift:

- Do not rewrite body prose inside appendix.
- Do not add new lore while building tables.
- Do not treat a candidate row as a confirmed entry.

This order tests the whole setting-book voice: world premise, continent, city, person, object, peoplehood, and evidence control.

## Red Flag Checklist

Before polishing any chapter, scan for:

- internal labels exposed in reader-facing paragraphs
- too many file names in body prose
- tables replacing emotional explanation
- uncertain candidates written as confirmed facts
- item lists without desire hooks
- place lists without route or conflict function
- non-human terms promoted without peoplehood evidence
- faction names that still sound too modern without intent

## Conductor Decision

The recomposition should now run in two tracks:

1. polished body samples that test reader experience
2. appendix samples that preserve verification discipline

If those two tracks stay separate, the final setting book can feel alive without losing control.
